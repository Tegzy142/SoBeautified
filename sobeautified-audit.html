Critical Issues
These are actively costing you clients and money right now
Fix Immediately
1
All product prices show ₦19.99
Every product — Detangling Brush, Lush Glam Braids, Olaplex No.7, Wigs — is showing ₦19.99. These are clearly default placeholder prices that were never updated. Any client who visits the shop and sees this will either lose trust or try to purchase at the wrong price.
Fix: Update all product prices immediately in Shopify admin
2
Search bar shows lorem ipsum placeholder text
When a visitor opens the search bar, the "Trending Now" section shows dummy words: "dempus", "magnis", "loremous saliduar", "naminos", "dinterdum". These are default Shopify template placeholders that were never removed. This looks broken and deeply unprofessional.
Fix: Remove or replace trending search terms in theme settings
3
Hero section has no text, headline or call to action
The homepage hero is just a large image with no headline, no tagline, and no button. The first thing a visitor sees tells them nothing about the brand. You have 3–5 seconds to capture attention — a blank hero wastes all of it.
Fix: Add a strong headline, short tagline, and "Book Now" button to the hero
4
All footer social media links are dead
Facebook, Instagram, Tumblr, and Twitter links in the footer all point to "#" — meaning they go nowhere. A client who wants to follow the brand on Instagram clicks and nothing happens. This is a missed opportunity on every page visit.
Fix: Add real social media URLs to all footer links
5
Hammam nav link goes to the Gallery page
Under "Our Services" in the navigation, clicking "Hammam" takes the visitor to the Gallery page — not a Hammam service page. This is a broken link that will confuse anyone interested in booking a Hammam service.
Fix: Update the Hammam nav link to point to the correct service page
6
Google Maps embed has a third-party watermark
The map on the site shows a visible "Embed Map on Website for Free — norsumediagroup.com" link. This is another brand's advertisement appearing on your site. It looks unfinished and cheapens the premium image the brand is going for.
Fix: Replace with a proper Google Maps embed without third-party branding
7
Copyright year shows 2022
The footer copyright reads "Copyright © 2022". It is now 2026. A site showing a 4-year-old copyright date looks abandoned and outdated — this erodes trust, especially for new visitors.
Fix: Update copyright year to 2026 in footer settings

Design Issues
These affect how professional and premium the brand looks
Important
8
Hero image is oversized on desktop
On larger screens the hero image is zoomed in too much — it fills the entire viewport with no breathing room and no context. It feels heavy and unbalanced compared to what a premium salon site should feel like.
Fix: Adjust hero image height and position to show more of the image at a comfortable scale
9
Announcement bar at the top should be removed
The purple/mauve announcement bar saying "Timeless Beauty. Personalised Touch. Welcome to So Beautified Lagos" feels outdated. It takes up valuable space and the "NG" country tag appears next to it unnecessarily, looking like a bug.
Fix: Remove the announcement bar or redesign it to be more minimal
10
About section body text is center-aligned
The paragraph text under "THE BEST BEAUTY SALON IN LAGOS" is centered. Long centered paragraphs are harder to read — research consistently shows left-aligned text is more readable for body copy. This reduces the professionalism of the section.
Fix: Change body paragraph text alignment to left
11
Service cards use circular images — feels dated
The circular image style for service cards (Braiding, Waxing, Facials, Nails) was popular in 2018–2020 but now looks outdated. Modern salon sites use full rectangular or square image cards with clean overlays. The current design undersells the quality of the services.
Fix: Replace circular image cards with full rectangular cards with proper image coverage
12
Individual service pages use a small circular image on one side
On service detail pages (e.g. Facials), the image appears as a small circle on the left with text beside it. The text below the image also feels disconnected and the content describes a body treatment under the Facials page — wrong description. The layout needs to be redesigned with a full-width hero image and properly written service content.
Fix: Redesign service pages with full-width imagery and correct content per service
🔵
Content Issues
These affect trust, clarity and conversion
Should Fix
13
Facials page has the wrong service description
The Facials page body text reads "Our warming body service is designed to exfoliate, open the pores..." — this is a body/Hammam description, not a facials description. Wrong content on a service page will confuse potential clients and damage credibility.
Fix: Write and upload correct content specific to facial treatments
14
No pricing information anywhere on the site
None of the service pages mention pricing ranges. Clients who don't know what to expect will either not book or go to a competitor who is more transparent. Even "Starting from ₦X,000" creates trust and reduces the back-and-forth inquiry messages.
Fix: Add price ranges or "from" pricing to each service page
15
Book Now and Contact Us go to the same page
"Book Now" and "Contact Us" in the navigation both link to the same Contact page. These should serve different purposes — booking should go to a dedicated booking form or system, while contact is for general enquiries. Having them merged creates confusion about what action to take.
Fix: Separate the booking flow from the general contact page

Next Steps

Start with the 7 critical issues — these are losing you clients today.
The design and content fixes can be addressed in order of priority after.
